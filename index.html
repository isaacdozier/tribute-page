Tribute to: Michael Faraday

"Nature is our kindest friend and best critic in experimental science if we only allow her intimations to fall unbiased on our minds."

Michael Faraday, 1791 – 1867
Michael Faraday was born to a poor family, but through determination he became known as one of his generations greatist scientific minds. His achievements dramatically changed civilization.

His most notable acheivements:

  • 1816 - Gives his first lecture to the City Philosophical Society.
  • 1821 - Discovers Electromagnetic Rotation and builds prototype to first electric motor.
  • 1823 - Discovers 'Gas Liquification', a method used in refrigeration.
  • 1825 - Discovers Benzene
  • 1831 - Discovers Electromagnetic Induction, a way of converting energy.
  • 1834 - Defines Faraday’s Laws of Electrolysis (used in battery production)

Read more about Michael Faraday at his Wikipedia page or famousscientists.org.
